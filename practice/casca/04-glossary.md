# Fintech / Banking Glossary

## Settlement
The completion of a payment or transaction when funds are legally or operationally finalized between parties.

## Reconciliation
The process of matching internal records against external bank or provider statements to confirm amounts and statuses.

## Ledger
A system of record for financial movement. In fintech, it often requires append-only, auditable records rather than in-place mutation.

## Double-entry accounting
A bookkeeping model where every financial event affects at least two accounts, preserving balance and audit integrity.

## Chargeback
A reversal initiated by a cardholder or issuer after a transaction has already been settled.

## KYC / AML
Know Your Customer and Anti-Money Laundering controls used to verify identity and detect suspicious activity.

## Underwriting
The process of evaluating a borrower or applicant for risk and deciding whether to approve, decline, or escalate review.

## Idempotency key
A unique client- or workflow-generated key that ensures the same request is not processed more than once even if retries occur.

## Ledger entry
A single atomic financial record in the ledger that represents a debit, credit, or associated metadata change.

## Reserve / authorization
A temporary hold on funds to ensure availability for a future transaction.

## Reversal
A cancellation or rollback of a completed or attempted financial event.

## Cutoff window
A business or operational time boundary after which transactions are batched, posted, or settled.

## ACH
Automated Clearing House; a batch-based U.S. payment network for transfers and debits.

## Exposure
The amount of risk or financial loss a system or institution faces from a customer, portfolio, or transaction.
